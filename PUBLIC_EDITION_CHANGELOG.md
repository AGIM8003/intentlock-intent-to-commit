# IntentLock v5.0 Public Edition Changelog

This edition consolidates the submitted v4.3, v4.4, and v4.5 material into one
public research specification.

## Major Upgrades

- Added author, publication number, abstract, keywords, citation, rights,
  research question, contributions, and AI-assistance disclosure.
- Added current public product and research comparisons.
- Added thirty official or primary references.
- Reframed novelty around the integrated intent-to-commit evidence chain.
- Separated authority, runtime containment, repository acceptance, and evidence.
- Replaced the scalar enforcement ladder with a capability vector.
- Reclassified Git diff verification as acceptance control, not host prevention.
- Replaced platform promises with testable Linux, Windows, and macOS profiles.
- Updated MCP, policy-engine, supply-chain, cryptographic, and regulatory positioning.
- Added a portable acceptance-boundary prototype specification.
- Repaired duplicate Test 56 by creating unique Tests 52-58.

## Removed From the Public Edition

- Internal numbering and single-source-of-truth statements.
- `50.X`, ProjectMind, and private framework bindings.
- Claims about an unpublished live verifier.
- The unverified machine-executed test receipt.
- Assertions that any operating-system profile or full product is already built.

## Preserved

- Intent Contract and contract revisions.
- Contract Compiler and Policy IR.
- shadow execution and write-ahead authorization.
- Diff Envelopes and two-phase planning.
- privacy, secret, dependency, egress, and prompt-injection gates.
- Kinetic Governor.
- independent merge, reversal, crash recovery, attestations, and proof capsules.
- falsification criteria, proof tests, benchmarks, roadmap, and decision locks.
